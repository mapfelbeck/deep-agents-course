## Experience

Experience is a key predictor of the level of technical proficiency for many target roles. Initial screens are used to explore the candidate’s relevant experience. Interviewers should seek the same types of information as is sought in the General Initial Interview Script, however the more advanced the role that is being interviewed for, the more open ended the questions should be. Strong, experienced candidates should be able to provide well rounded and reasoned responses with less/minimal guidance from the interviewer.

## Technical Proficiency

To the extent relevant for the target role, the initial interview validates the candidate’s self\-proclaimed strengths and summarizes relevant weaknesses.

In the area that the candidate claims to be strongest, failing to answer any two fundamental questions yields a failing overall assessment. Beyond that, all candidates should have an intermediate level of mastery of at least some skills. Architect\-level candidates should have intermediate skill level in most skill areas and advanced mastery of multiple skill areas.

## Consulting Presence

Candidates should demonstrate increasingly strong consulting presence based on the seniority of the role for which they are interviewing. This manifests in the candidates ability to clearly communicate ideas or represent concepts. They should seek clarity when appropriate. Ideally, they represent themselves as confident learners.

## Skill Areas

To assess the candidate’s technical fitness for the target role and specialization, the tech interviewer will assess breadth and depth in key skill areas. Depending on the target role and specialization, a tech interview for software developers typically assess most of the ten skill areas below.

Architecture (client\-server, messaging, scale, fault tolerance...): Tech Interview Questions: Expertise\=Solution Architecture"

* "Describe the architecture of an interesting project solution you helped build."


	+ Candidate ideally gives concise, meaningful descriptions of networking, messaging, protocols....
* "What improvements would you make to any interesting solution you've helped build?"
* "Do you have experience in micro\-service architectures?"


	+ "How do you decide what’s in a micro\-service, what are its boundaries?"
	+ "Do you have communication between services / how is that handled?"
	+ "How would you handle async requests?"
* "What are some of the benefits to a serverless architecture?"

Methodology (agile, SDLC, requirements analysis, technical documentation, prototyping…): Tech Interview Questions: Expertise\=Software development methodology

* "What software development methodologies have been employed in your projects?"


	+ Candidate ideally gives range of methodologies (e.g. agile to waterfall) with brief notes of variations from standard practices.
* "What methodology changes would you recommend for any software development organization you've worked with?"
* "How do you break down and estimate the work for a developer or developer team?"

  


Object\-oriented design (classes, methods, encapsulation scope, visibility, inheritance...): Tech Interview Questions: Expertise\=Object\-oriented design

* "What classes would you create to represent \[chess, pastries to 3D print, dog boarding...]?"


	+ Candidate ideally asks for clarification of solution parameters and makes a model that's just complex enough.

Programming languages (Java, Objective C, JavaScript, HTML, CSS, PHP...): Tech Interview Questions: Expertise\=Programming languages

* "Make a widget appear 30 seconds after the \[page, screen, view] appears."

  


Platforms, and frameworks (Android, iOS, JavaScript, .NET...): Tech Interview Questions: Expertise\=Frameworks

* "When does a method belong in a View vs a Controller?"

  


Data (SQL, NoSQL, XML, JSON, ORM…): Tech Interview Questions: Expertise\=Data

* "When and how should a database be partitioned?"


	+ Candidate ideally mentions both horizontal and vertical partitioning in appropriate scenarios.

  


Services (web, REST, SOA, no\-UI processes, asynchronicity, networks, protocols, versioning. Tech Interview Questions: Expertise\=Services

* "Distinguish SOA from REST. When is each relevant?"

  


Software design patterns (MVC, factory, command...): Tech Interview Questions: Expertise\=Software design patterns

* "When would you have a class expose a delegate?"

Generative AI (Prompting, RAG, Fine Tuning…): Tech Interview Questions: Expertise\=GenAI

* “What factors would you focus on when choosing between RAG or Fine Tuning for a use case?”

Tools and techniques (e.g. source control, test first, optimize last, code deployment, refactoring, debugging, instrumentation…): Tech Screen Questions: Expertise\=Tools

* "How to diagnose an out\-of\-memory exception?"

  


Algorithms (recursion, time/memory optimization...): Tech Interview Questions: Expertise\=Algorithms

* "Select two small videos to play in series while a main video plays. (E.g. two 30 second videos to match a 60 second main video.)"


	+ Candidate ideally refines the question and discusses optimization options.

  


### Assessment

Each assessment should include a summary of the candidate’s strongest relevant skills and their overall strength/suitability for the target role, e.g.:

\[Candidate] \[failed/passed/is] a \[novice/intermediate/strong/expert] \[role, e.g. “iOS developer” or “enterprise Java developer”] specializing in \[technology or other specialization, e.g. “native Android apps”].

Typically, the assessment will also include the candidate’s strengths with the relevant background technologies, e.g. “ \[ candidate] has a \[no/limited/considerable/extensive] software development background, including experience with \[BA/PM/dev/lead/architect] roles.... \[candidate] has a \[weak/decent/strong] understanding of \[skill area 1, 2, 3\...]. \[candidate] did a \[poor/passable/great] job answering \[...] except for \[...]. For example, ....”
