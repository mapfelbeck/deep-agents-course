# Software Engineering - Behavioral Interview  (Moved to Loop)

# How to use this question bank

The behavioral interview is our opportunity to build an understanding for how a candidate responds to various situations while examining their approach to the types work and environments that we typically encounter. We use this to determine a candidate fit beyond their technical proficiency. We are interested in *core values and behaviors* that are crucial to success in Software Engineering within a professional services organization. These interviews are an opportunity to dig into a candidate’s Consulting Presence, Persistence \& Curiosity, Teaming \& Collaboration style and their approach to Continuous Growth.

This question bank contains a lot of questions and represents the competencies and skills that successful candidates will exhibit based on our Career Development Framework. 

*Do not try to use all of the questions in an interview.* 

**Our current process incorporates two behavioral interviews. Prior to your behavioral interview**, the two behavioral interviewers should coordinate on selecting questions across competencies and skills. This will ensure wider coverage for candidate evaluations. You should **select a subset of questions that align to the role** and level that the candidate is interviewing for. Remember, an early career individual contributor is going to have different perspective and experience than a seasoned leader.

For the best results, revisit this question bank frequently in order to familiarize yourself with the types of behavioral qualities that we are looking for in order to ascertain whether a candidate is ready for the role they are interviewing for or not. If you have suggestions for new questions please email \[**should we allow submissions?]**

Take your time with the candidate and remember, the key to this conversation is to leave with an assessment of whether the candidate will be successful at slalom in the role that they are interviewing for. We need to know that they will be able to contribute in a rich, team environment with competing view points and priorities.
