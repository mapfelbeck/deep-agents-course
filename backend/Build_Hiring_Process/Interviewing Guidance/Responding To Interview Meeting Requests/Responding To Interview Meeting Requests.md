# Responding To Interview Meeting Requests

Coordinating schedules of busy interviewers and eager candidates is a challenge. There are many things that we, as interviewers, can do to ensure that this process is as smooth as possible. Often, candidates are working in a compressed timeline, so your flexibility is a key contributor to our ability to schedule interviews successfully. Always respond to requests for interviews as promptly as you are able to! Remember: as a member of the interviewers pool, you are responsible for being an excellent partner with the TA community. Challenges with your schedule will always come up \- an unplanned client meeting, illness, or simply not having an up\-to\-date calendar will at some point result in a schedule clash. This page offers best practices to minimize difficulties in scheduling interviews.

# Calendars

TA Organizers will respect your calendar and as a general rule will not schedule over a meeting in your calendar. The first step toward streamlining interview scheduling is to ensure that your Slalom Outlook calendar is up to date. 

* This may require manually syncing externally managed client calendar events into your Slalom calendar
* Syncing goes two ways: If you have a client\-hosted calendar, you should be sure to add the interview as a ‘Slalom Internal’ meeting in your client\-hosted calendar. 


	+ Mark yourself as out of office so anyone from the client knows that you are unavailable
* Make sure you create private appointments for events that will require you to be out of office \- such as doctor visits or PTO \- and mark them as out of office. 


	+ If you work with a particular TA specialist, please do include them if you create a non\-blocking appointments representing your PTO

# Responding to Interview Requests

Please use the following guidelines when responding to interview requests:

* Strive to respond to meeting requests for interviews within a day. 


	+ Please remember to always “Send a response” when responding to the outlook calendar invite
* If you have no conflicts, this should be relatively straightforward to accept the interview.
* If you have any concerns with accepting the interview, please reach out to the TA organizer via Slack to get your concerns answered 


	+ e.g.: *“Hi Jenna: I have never done a Behavioral Screen, can I shadow on first?”* or *“I am a Sr. Principal and it looks like you are asking me to talk to a Director candidate \- why?”*
* If you have a known conflict, evaluate if that conflict can be rescheduled


	+ This won’t always be possible, but if it is, then it makes for a better scheduling experience for the candidate
* If a conflict cannot be rescheduled, offer up alternative times that could work for you to have the interview


	+ e.g. *“Hi Jake: I cannot make it to this interview on Monday at 1\. Could the candidate chat at 2? I could also meet on Tuesday from 10 to noon”*

# Schedule Clashes or Illness

Typically, TA Organizers will look for slots that are free on your calendar. A schedule clash will occur when you have a late\-scheduled meeting added to your calendar. A similar situation arises if you become ill. These unavoidable clashes will eventually come up. It is important for interviewers to keep TA Organizers informed and up to date when these occur so they can work with the candidate appropriately.

* First: protect interviews that are booked. If another meeting is booked on top of an existing interview, you should try to move the new meeting first.
* Assuming the clash is not moveable, it is up to you to communicate with the TA Organizer in order to find a suitable replacement.
* Contact the TA Organizer straight away: let them know that you’ve had an immovable conflict come up and you won’t be able to do the interview. 


	+ Again, if you are able, offer up some alternative times that might work
	
	
		- e.g. *“Hi Hannah: I’ve had an emergency stakeholder meeting scheduled on top of the interview I was scheduled for 10am. I could meet the candidate this afternoon, if they are able to. Otherwise, can you help me find a replacement?”*
* Always await an acknowledgement from the TA Organizer


	+ Reach out to the TA organizer through as many channels as possible \- slack, MS Teams, Outlook or even via the classic telephone until you get a response.
	
	
		- If the organizer is out of office, you must get in contact with their team.
	+ Do not fire off an outlook ‘Decline’ into the ether and assume it’s handled.

**Remember**: You are working in partnership with the TA community. A good partner will work together through scheduling difficulties to come to a reasonable outcome all while ensuring that the candidate has an excellent interviewing experience.
