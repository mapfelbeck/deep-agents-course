**Question**: What aspects or elements of waterfall and traditional technology project management do you think are helpful to running an agile development team for a client?

**Expected responses:** 

1. Weekly status reporting
2. Roadmap planning
3. Budget tracking
4. Risk logs and mitigation practices
5. Issue logs and problem solving
6. Kanban boards for managing story development towards DoR
7. Team calendars for OOO

**Response Rating**: None / Weak / Fair / Good / Strong / Excellent

**Notes/Comments:**

**Question**: How do you make sure that what’s being built in high\-quality? What indicators or data points do you look at? What quality practices would you expect to be used, or insist on, in one of your projects?

**Response Rating**: None / Weak / Fair / Good / Strong / Excellent

**Notes/Comments:**

**Expected responses:**

Look for quality checks at multiple stages \- requirements, development, testing, exploratory testing, regression testing, non\-functional requirements (including performance) testing.

Look for getting feedback from users and stakeholders as you go.

For indicators, look for things like sprint stability, points committed vs. delivered over time, unit test coverage, passed vs. failed builds, etc.

For practices, look for test automation, Test\-Driven Development (TDD),, CI/CD practices, collective code ownership, measurement/monitoring, automated recovery.
