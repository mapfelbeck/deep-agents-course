# Solution Ownership - Behavioral Interview

Solution Ownership – Behavioral Interviewing (i.e. finding the purple unicorns) 

 

The SO team believes that hiring great Solution Owners is a balance between Technical and Behavioral skills. Finding the right people means assessing multiple qualities. As a team we have put together the following document to lead our Behavioral interviewing. The questions in the first list are MUST ASK so that we get a consistent screening of candidates. In the subsequent sections are additional questions that the team has developed and that screeners can add as appropriate. At the bottom of the document is the working notes that lead the team to develop these specific questions. 

 

Use this document to take your notes during the interview then copy/paste everything in Section 1 into the JobScience mail.

Section 3 \- Behavioral Questions Background Notes 

 

Behavior categories: 

* Adaptability
* How well can the candidate handle an ever\-changing environment?
* How do they respond to stressors?
* Do they have a toolkit for navigating ambiguity and uncertainty?
* Self\-Awareness
* How does the candidate handle feedback?
* What do they do when they are wrong?
* What do they do when they make a mistake?
* What do they feel her strengths are?
* Are they willing to have tough but necessary conversations?
* Communication
* Do they have demonstrated experience communicating with lots of different levels of people and lots of different types of information (good and bad)?
* Can they navigate a difficult conversation?
* Can they tell a compelling story or vision?
* Can they be persuasive?
* Leadership
* Are they a thought leader?   A Group leader?
* Have they led people they didn’t directly manage?
* What do they feel are the most important qualities of leadership?
* Do they lead by example?
* Team Focus
* Can they motivate a team?
* Can they course\-correct a team?
* Collaboration
* Can they build collaborative relationships?
* Can they build strong rapport with clients and co\-workers?
* Are they a team player?
* Execution and Drive
* Can they consistently deliver results across different kinds of projects and situations?
* Do they come to the table with creative problem\-solving skills and a desire to constantly look for ways to get the job done despite obstacles?
* Achievement Oriented
* They are a self\-starter, taking initiative with a commitment to improve.
* Enjoys competing against a high standard of excellence.
* Client \& Service Driven
* They have a strong desire to help or serve others
* They take action to meet stakeholder needs and exceed expectations

Ideas for Interview process: 

* Could have 2 people sit in the Behavioral interviews.  This could help validate what was being heard and whether the desired or un\-desired responses were seen.
* Try to incorporate a well\-balanced and diverse team in the behavioral interview process to obtain a well\-rounded perspective
* Could use the Solution Architects to listen for specific issues/keywords and leverage as interview training.
* Think about having SO trained in “Widening the Circle” training.
* Could be part of the SO Summit (2 hours needed).
* PC\+ Summit could do “Covering” goes into unconscious bias (NB this needs more thought)
* Think about ensuring all interviewers have had Diversity training.

 

 

Behavioral Issues Seen (that require screening questions)  

* Lack of self\-awareness
* Set in their ways
* Low/No drive
* Low personality; non engaging
* Victim mode
* High Drama
* Low maturity
* High Self\-Orientation
* Aggressive
* Over sharing personal/gossip
* Low EQ
* No client Service mindset
* Agile by the book
* Only individual contributor experience – not a team player

 

We’re concentrating on having questions that target all bullets above the line.
