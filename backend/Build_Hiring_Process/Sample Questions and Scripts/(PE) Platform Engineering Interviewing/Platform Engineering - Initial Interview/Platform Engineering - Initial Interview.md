# Platform Engineering - Initial Interview

This is intended as a loose guide for conducting an initial interview of a potential PE candidate.  

Throughout the interview, you're looking to evaluate the candidate's communication skills:

* Can they clearly express their thoughts?
* Do they use examples or data to explain their ideas?
* How well could they explain complex concepts to their peers (as part of a team)?

A perfect candidate can not only explain HOW to do something but also WHY doing something a certain way is important/efficient etc.

The scale used for evaluating candidates is:



|  | Rating | Meaning |
| --- | --- | --- |
| 0 | Not Covered | You didn't talk about the subject, or the subject was not applicable. (If giving this rating, please include a brief phrase why, i.e. "Not covered (ran out of time)" |
| 1 | Poor | Consistently and/or significantly missed expectations for the role for which they are interviewing. This rating is also applicable if the candidate has no knowledge in an area. |
| 2 | Fair | Sometimes met and sometimes missed expectations for the role for which they are interviewing |
| 3 | Good | Consistently met and sometimes exceeded expectations for the role for which they are interviewing |
| 4 | Excellent | Consistently and/or significantly exceeded expectations for the role for which they are interviewing |

If the recruiter has specified that the candidate may be straddling two roles, be sure to specify in your [writeup](https://slalom.atlassian.net/wiki/pages/resumedraft.action?draftId=68059453#SoftwareEngineering-PhoneScreen-Writeup) which role you assessed the candidate against; the Rating you assign should be based on the role which you graded the candidate against. For example, if the candidate was possibly an Engineer or a Sr. Engineer, but in talking to them you determined they were a better fit as an Engineer. In that case, in your writeup specify that your Ratings are for the Engineer role because that's where you feel the candidate is the best fit.
