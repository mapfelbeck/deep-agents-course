# Platform Engineering - Technical Interview

## **Objectives of the Technical Interview for Platform Engineering**

The objective of the Technical Interview is to assess the overall skills competency of the candidate based on the following:

1. Their ability to solve problems, ask the right questions, think about things in a cohesive, structured manner and communicate effectively.
2. Their knowledge of Cloud services, Cloud Architecture, and Cloud Best Practices.
3. Their understanding and experience with Continuous Integration/Continuous Deployment (CI/CD), DevOps tooling and pipelines in particular.
4. Their understanding and experience with Infrastructure as Code (IaC).
5. Experience with or understanding of containers, container orchestration, Kubernetes.
6. General understanding of Security considerations with respect to Cloud, Pipelines, Network and Software Engineering.
7. Familiarity with modern software development methodologies, Software Development Life Cycle (SDLC), tools and practices.

NOTE: Candidates ability to go deep on any or all of these topics are highly dependent on level, time in industry and work experience. It’s important for a screener to recognize the level of the individual they are speaking with and tailor their interviewing to that. We don’t expect junior engineering candidates to span all skills, tools and capabilities while we expect Sr. Architects to have a deep toolkit across many if not all of these areas.

#### **Engineer / Senior Engineer (Velocity Roles)**

For Engineers and Senior Engineers, we are assessing the candidate on their ability to perform effectively on a team. Our primary focus here is to determine whether they can effectively contribute to velocity on the projects we work on; in parallel, we should also be evaluating whether this is someone we can work with. 

#### **Architect Roles (Architect / Senior Architect / Senior Principal / Director)**

For Architect\+ roles, we are assessing the candidate on their ability to lead a development team and drive architecture design on projects. We are expecting these candidates to be able to go both deep (strong technical expertise in Cloud, DevOps and Security concepts) and wide (broad understanding of technologies across the architecture disciplines). Their ability to lead, and confidence in communicating architectural concepts and decisions need to be reflected throughout the interview process.

## **Interview Structure**

### **Introduction (5 mins)**

The Interviewer should first explain the interview process, explain the shadowing practice we use at Slalom and introduce the person shadowing.

Interviewer should introduce themselves and give a brief overview of who they are, their role at Slalom and overall industry experience.

The Introduction is to build rapport, put the candidate at ease, and set clear expectations on the flow of the interview (ie. “*We have an aggressive schedule, and we’ll need to move through the sections quickly so don’t be discouraged if i cut you off…*”). For more context, see Technical Interview.

### **Problem Solving Questions (30\+ mins)**

The structure of the interview will not vary much based on the role/level the candidate is being interviewed for. Typically we will explore the same subject areas with all candidates, but seek deeper, more detailed and complete responses from the more senior level candidates.

**Cloud Solutioning Problem (15\-20 mins)** 

Determine the candidate’s ability to solve a problem or propose a solution to a desired outcome using Cloud Services. Propose a scenario with a few basic requirements and have the candidate describe how they would solve that problem and what Cloud Services they would use to do it. In this exercise, we are assessing the following:

* What is the candidate’s ability to organize their thoughts and use appropriate services for things like structured and unstructured data storage; inconsistent load (scalability); data processing; edge availability; network connectivity and isolation.
* Does the candidate understand databases and what types are appropriate in various scenarios and for structured vs. unstructured data?
* Does the candidate understand managed vs. unmanaged services and the tradeoffs between them?
* Can the candidate articulate the pros and cons of one or more serverless offerings?
* Is the candidate familiar with concepts such as scalability, availability, durability, redundancy and are they able to apply them or discuss them in the context of a cloud solution.
* Can the candidate articulate the value proposition of Cloud Computing?
* Does the candidate understand cloud cost tradeoffs and have a sense of cost optimization in a cloud environment?

**CI/CD Discussion (5 \- 10 mins)**

Determine the candidate’s understanding and experience with DevOps Pipelines and CI/CD.

* Is the candidate familiar with Jenkins, Azure Dev Ops or other popular pipeline engines?
* Does the candidate understand automation in general?
* What are the advantages of using pipelines?
* What types of actions can take place or be initiated via a pipeline?
* What is the difference between CI and CD?

**IaC Coding Problem (10 \- 15 mins)** 

Determine the candidate’s ability to solve an IaC coding problem. It is highly encouraged that the candidate solve the problem using a program language that we typically use (ie. Terraform, CloudFormation, Python) \- avoid using pseudo\-code. In this exercise, we are assessing:

* How quickly, correctly and effectively can the candidate write code?
* Can the candidate think creatively, out of the box, and adapt to dynamic situations?
* Can the candidate explain their thought process clearly, and justify decisions they are making?
* Is the candidate asking clarifying questions, understanding constraints?
* Does the candidate understand “state”, “persistence”, “immutability”, “ephemerality”, “idempotence”?

### Optional Discussion Topics based on candidate role and time remaining

**Containers/Kubernetes Discussion (5 \- 10 mins)**

Determine the candidate’s understanding and experience with Containers and/or Kubernetes.

* Has the candidate worked with containers?
* Can the candidate articulate the value proposition for container use?
* Has the candidate worked with Kubernetes or other container orchestration systems?
* Can the candidate describe the reasons why you would want to use a container orchestration system?
* Is the candidate familiar with Service Mesh?
* Can the candidate describe the reasons why you would use a service mesh?

**Security Discussion (5 \- 10 mins)**

Determine the candidate’s understanding of Security as it applies to Cloud and DevOps.

* Is the candidate familiar with the concept of “DevSecOps”?
* Can the candidate describe the “Shared Responsibility Model”?
* Is the candidate familiar with principle of “least privilege”?
* Guide a general discussion of security best practices when deploying basic cloud services.
* Discuss how “security” can be integrated into the CI/CD pipeline.

**Modern software development methodologies, Software Development Life Cycle (SDLC), tools and practices Discussion (5 \- 10 mins)**

Determine the candidate’s understanding and experience with software development.

* Can the candidate describe a SDLC?
* Does the candidate know Git?
* Is the candidate familiar with Open Source Technologies?
* Can the candidate articulate the pros and cons of leveraging Open Source?
* Is the candidate familiar with Agile Methodologies?
* Is the candidate familiar with QA/QE and software testing in general?

### **Candidate Questions and Next Steps (10 \- 15 mins)**

* Provide the candidate an opportunity to ask questions. For more context, see Technical Interview.
* Wrap up conversation and conduct debrief or close interview if you are the last with instructions on next steps.
