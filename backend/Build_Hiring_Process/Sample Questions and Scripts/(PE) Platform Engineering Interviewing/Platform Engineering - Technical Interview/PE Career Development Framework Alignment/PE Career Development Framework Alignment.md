# PE Career Development Framework Alignment

The interview questions we ask are designed to measure a candidates knowledge and ability to the criteria required to be successful within build. They are also a way to measure a candidates technical level within our roles as we have defined them in our Career Development Framework. [(CDF)](https://twodegrees1.sharepoint.com/teams/Build/SitePages/Career-Framework.aspx)

### The levels in CDF are defined as a 1\-5 scale as follows:



| **Level** | **Description** | **Definition** | **Example** |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| 1 | Beginner | Someone operating at a beginner level in various skills or capabilities will generally be fairly ‘shallow and slim’: They likely know trivial answers to conceptual questions assuming they are familiar with the concept at all. Additionally, they will not have started to form the associations with related concepts. They can influence an assigned scope of work. | A PE engineer is a beginner level when they can write and maintain Cloud Formation or Terraform code. |
| 2 | Foundational | Someone at the foundational level with a skill will be able to help others perform that skill. They are starting to develop some depth in particular skills, but that depth will not be met by other skills. They can articulate different flavors of a concept. They can influence their assigned scope of work while also lending a hand to other team mates. | A PE engineer has developed a foundational level when they are able to define their own repos and working CF or Terraform code modules and they are able to help their team mates do the same. |
| 3 | Intermediate | Someone at the intermediate level with a skill has a good understanding of that particular concept and they are starting to draw correlations between that concept and other concepts. They are able to guide a team through an aggregate scope of work | A PE engineer is developing an intermediate level of understanding when they are able to understand the tradeoffs between different cloud services when multiple services could accomplish the same thing. |
| 4 | Advanced | Someone at an advanced level with a skill is developing a depth and breadth of that concept. They can articulate different approaches to reach the same goal and they understand the overlapping influences and impacts of related concepts. They are able to direct multiple work streams through an aggregated scope of work toward consistent application of patterns and practices | A PE engineer is developing advanced skills when they are able to effectively communicate the benefits of particular architectural implementations and accurately describe the pros and cons of various architectural decisions on increasingly complex systems. |
| 5 | Expert | When operating at an advanced level with a skill, the team member has breadth and depth across many different concepts. They are able to draw from that breadth to understand the impacts of decisions between various concepts. They are able to influence the strategy and direction across multiple projects. | A PE engineer has expert level skills when they can plan, articulate to the team and implement a multi\-faceted solution to a complex architectural problem; design appropriate CI/CD pipelines; be proficient in Infrastructure as Code (IaC); and implement security best practices. |
