# Step 4: Lead Live Interview

After the trainee has shadowed a live interview, the final step will be to lead a live interview or “reverse shadow”. This is where the trainee will run the interview on their own and an experienced interviewer will observe to provide feedback. 

**Before the interview:**

* The trainee should review the candidate’s evaluation packet to get familiar with the candidate, think through a plan of action on how they are going to conduct the interview, including what questions they plan to ask.

**During the interview:**

* The new interviewer/trainee leads the interview and takes notes
* The experienced interviewer can introduce themselves at the beginning, but then turn off their camera and go on mute so there are no distractions during the interview. The experienced interviewer will quietly observe, but should jump in if needed to steer the interview in the right direction.

**After the interview:**

* The new interviewer/trainee will take their notes and create a write\-up with feedback following our evaluation guidelines and submit into Jobscience within 24 hours. This step is really important!
* The new interviewer can set up an informal meeting/debrief with the experienced interviewer to discuss the quality of interview questions, quality of feedback, and any other tips on how to conduct interviews moving forward.
* The new interviewer is now officially certified for the interview stage completed!
