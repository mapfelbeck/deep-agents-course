# Step 3: Shadow Live Interview

The goal of shadowing a live interview is for the **interviewer in training** to learn the overall format of the interview in a live, interactive environment. This step allows the shadower/trainee to listen, observe, and learn from other experienced interviewers, as well as have a consistent frame of reference of what a “good” interview should look like. 

#### The Shadower/Trainee Role:

* Once the shadower is scheduled to shadow an interview, the recruiter will send the candidate’s evaluation packet via an encrypted email. Please review the candidate’s resume/LinkedIn profile and previous feedback evaluations in advance of the interview to get familiar with the candidate.
* The recruiter always lets the candidate know that a shadower will be joining the interview, but will not be actively participating in it. The shadower should introduce themselves and say hello in the beginning to make the candidate feel welcome and comfortable, but then go off camera and on mute to not cause any distractions while the interview is taking place.
* During the live interview, the shadowers: **listen and observe.** Take notes and practice filling out an evaluation write\-up for the criteria being assessed.
* After the interview, the interviewer and shadower debrief for about 10\-15 minutes and compare and discuss ratings. 


	+ During the debrief, the shadower can ask any questions they may have to the interviewer to learn more about the overall interview.
* The experienced interviewer will submit their evaluation for the candidate within 24 hours (the shadower’s notes are meant for practice).
