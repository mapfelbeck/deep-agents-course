# Overview

Miro is a whiteboarding tool that allows multiple participants to interactively participate in drawing, diagramming and rudimentary pseudo\-coding activities.

In April 2021, slalom build selected Miro as the preferred tool to replace AwwApp for the following reasons:

* Ease of candidate experience: a new Miro board can be created and shared with a candidate via a simple web link.


	+ There is no need for the candidate or the interviewer to sign up for access to the tool
* Private and anonymous data management in alignment with IT Security policy
* The tool provides a very straightforward user experience that we believe will ‘get out of the way’ of candidates so they can focus on their interview and not on the tool


	+ Basic shapes are intuitive to use and behave in a consistent manner
	+ Freehand drawing is clean and easy to use
	+ Textboxes and labeling tools are consistent and easy to use
	+ Effective ‘snap\-to\-' functionality
	+ Connections are persistent allowing for drawing manipulation
* Cost


	+ Miro has a per\-seat cost for interviewers and coordinators.

A selection team went through extensive evaluation of several tools and Miro was the clear winner providing the best user experience, consistent with or better than the experience with AwwApp.

**What is Miro?** 

Miro is an enterprise\-grade collaboration tool built around the concept of a whiteboard. Many project teams use it as a collaboration space to collaborate in ways that are common with a physical whiteboard:

* Drawing
* Stickie note collaboration
* Design

**How does a candidate gain access to this enterprise system?**

We have worked with IT to create a team within Miro that has the ability to create anonymous links to individual whiteboards. These links allow recipients to open the whiteboard without needing to log in or provide Miro credentials. 

**How will interview whiteboards be managed?**

**How are whiteboards created?**

When a recruiter schedules a virtual technical interview, they will create a whiteboard in the TA team. 

**How are whiteboards shared?**

The recruiter scheduling a virtual interview will include a link to the whiteboard in the meeting invite. The link will be sent to the candidate as well as the interviewer.

**How are whiteboards cleaned up?**

The TA team will clean up boards after the interviews are completed

**I am in an interview and the candidate did not receive a link to a whiteboard. How can I get one to share with them?**

* Click the share button in the upper right hand corner
* Click the ‘Copy board link’ button
* Paste the link into chat in the MS Teams meeting for the interview

**How do I get access to the TA Team within Miro?**

Currently only the TA team will access to this group and will generate and share the links with the recruiters as needed. This will evolve along with our approach.

**Has Miro been approved for use by IT/Security for this purpose?**

Anonymous links from Miro have been approved for use by IT for the purposes of supporting the interview process. All other use cases for anonymous whiteboard sharing must coordinate with IT Security for approval.
