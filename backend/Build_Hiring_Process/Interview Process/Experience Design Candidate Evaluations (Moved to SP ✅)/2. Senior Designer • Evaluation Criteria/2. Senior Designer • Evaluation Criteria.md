# 2. Senior Designer • Evaluation Criteria

### **STEP THREE:**Identify where you want to dig deeper in the next steps. Take note that it is NOT expected that someone will check all boxes. If they are exceeding many of the expectations provided here, consider evaluating them as an **[Architect](https://slalom.atlassian.net/wiki/pages/resumedraft.action?draftId=2880438586)**



| **Senior Designer Summary** | **Outward Facing Job Title:** “Senior Product Designer” |
| --- | --- |
| * ***Must* have some professional design experience** (production setting, freelance, or adjacent design stack). * Could be a design career pivot (2\-5\+ years) / bootcamp graduate. * Actively applying design fundamentals and solid UI/visual skills * Understands digital product design and how to work with tooling. Be able to manage the end\-to\-end design process for digital prototypes and whatever assets are needed to support user stories. * Needs to be able to be autonomous, lead a delivery pod without oversight and/or be able to support discovery activities with a Sr. Architect\+ | |

:frame\_photo:1f5bc🖼\#F4F5F7**Reminder about portfolio \& skills screens:**

* Transferable skills from marketing, environmental, architectural, industrial, or other related fields of design and engineering experience should be considered. Look for potential in their ability to solve problems through design over proof of specific deliverables.
* You may see evidence of a career shift—this is ok, so long as they have demonstrated proficiency in product design
* Portfolio materials can be in many forms including but not limited to website, pdf, presentation, dribble, behance, social platform feed, etc. Consider whether they have used good judgement, attention to detail, and curation in the demonstration of work.
