# Initial Interview

# Overview

The Initial Interview (formerly known as the technical phone screen, or phone screen interview), is an initial in\-depth conversation between a candidate and a discipline\-specific interviewer and should provide broad indicators as to a candidate’s technical proficiency. During the initial interview, the interviewer is asking questions across a variety of technical topics in order to assess the candidate’s ability to perform the duties required of the role they are interviewing for. 

Keep in mind that this conversation has much significance in portraying Slalom as a caring, human\-centric workplace. This conversation should be a challenge for the candidate, but we are never aiming to trip up the candidate or ask them trick questions.

This page provides general guidance for the Initial Interview process that are consistent across discipline areas.   discipline specific processes, scripts and interview guidance links are provided in each section where appropriate.

# Conversation

As the interviewer progresses through the interview, it’s important to keep in mind the following two objectives:

* **Determine technical proficiency.**  Does the candidate understand the role expectations? Can the candidate perform effectively in the role they’re interviewing for?
* **Provide the best possible candidate experience.** Candidates are investing time to assess fit with us as well \- are we giving our candidates the **best interview experience** possible, reflecting our Slalom Values throughout our process?

The initial interview conversation adheres to the following flow:



|  | **Agenda** | **Activities** | **Outcome** | **Suggested Time Limit** |
| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |
| 1 | **Introduction** | 1. Introduce yourself and your role at Slalom 2. Provide a high level agenda for the call 3. Set expectations on 1\. the level of detail the candidate should get into, and 2\. the pace of the interview. 4. Ask the candidate to introduce themselves. \**Given time sensitivity, time box the response and be specific \- ie. passion for the field, most recent experience.*  \* Use the introduction as an opportunity to reduce interview jitters, and build rapport with the candidate. | * Clear expectations on the format of the call * Clear expectations on the level of detail a candidate should provide. | 5\-10 minutes |
| 2 | **Technical Deep Dive** | 1. Deep dive into the outlined core competency map 2. Perform a technical evaluation of: 	1. Fundamental Concepts 	2. Problem Solving 	3. Best Practice Usage | * A determination of the candidate’s technical proficiency to perform effectively in the role. | 30\-40 minutes |
| 3 | **Candidate Questions \& Next Steps** | 1. Provide opportunity for candidate to ask questions about the role and team 2. Set expectations on next steps of the Interview Process | * Assess fit / motivation based on candidate questions * Sell candidate on the opportunity | 5\-10 minutes |

# Feedback

We use the same Assessment Summary email template for all screens (initial, skills and behavioral).

A slight nuance is that with a shorter time scheduled in an initial screen, we are more focused on trying to make a decision on whether the candidate should be brought in for continued interviews based on their technical proficiency.

The skills and behavioral interviews, on the other hand, assess the likelihood of the individual being able to deliver development velocity close to day 1\.

Staffability and potential for development velocity on day 1 is a litmus test that all candidates should pass. As an example, it may seem hard to pass up on smart person who is great with Mathematics. But, it is best that we do if they have not been doing what we do here and would need ramp up to deliver development velocity.

Every one of us will be learning everyday on the job, but for the most part, the expectation is that those investments can be made while providing useful velocity on projects.
