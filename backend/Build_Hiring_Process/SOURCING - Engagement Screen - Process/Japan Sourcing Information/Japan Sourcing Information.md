**LinkedIn Webinar for Japan**

* More jobs open than applicants applying
* Even if a candidate puts “open to work” they are just understanding the marketing and not actually “open to work”
* 56 touch points in a regular cycle is required
* 15% LinkedIn response rate is fantastic
* Market Leaders in specific industry are followed – we might want our leaders to really be out in the community/thought leaders
* Japanese candidates prefers casual meeting
* KISS  \- keep it short and simple when messaging candidates (best for engagement)
* Employer branding needs a lot of focus (especially branding on LinkedIn company page)
* Make sure your employer branding is mobile device/smart phone friendly
* Would be great to for Hiring Managers to be involved in messaging
* Best time to message is during the week between 1pm – 6pm
* Japanese candidates love to see their future colleagues and what the company represents
* In your headline on LinkedIn having Japanese language in Linkedin headline

 **Question \& Answer**


	+ **How long does the interview process take usually in Japan? ( Just wanted to clarify if it is anything different from Aus process and time)**  
	  
	**Answer:** Interview processes can take a little longer than normal due to the casual screens and "courting" that happens at the early stages. Once things get going, they can go as quickly as we go anywhere else.   
	Historically, timezones and non\-Japan based interviews have slowed things down a bit
	+ **Are all the clients local?**  
	**Answer:** Not all projects are required to be based in Japan, but that is the trend we are seeing \- lots of potential work using Alliance networks for global projects based out of Japan
	+ **How big is the entire Japan's team size including Local and Build?**   
	**Answer:** Last I checked we have about 120 \- but we've had some more signatures so it might be closer to 130 \- Build is clocking in at around 25\~30% of the team
