# End to End Process for Talent Engagement

Process Map for TE 

**Initial Meeting \- Hiring Priorities**   
  
Once a month  re\-occurring 1 hour meeting collaboration catch up with Talent Acquisition Manager (TAM)/Hiring Manager from designated function to provide update on pipeline and feedback 

Talent Engagement (TE) proactively source for current/future state.   
  
Based of the demand capacity report we can figure out if we want the candidate to be in the full end to end process or if this is more for future pipelining. 

**Two type of Evaluation Screens** 

**Sourcing Screen VS Recruiter Screen**

We will be using the **“Sourcing Screen”** for pipelining/networking purposes only.  

Whilst a **“Recruiter Screen”** will be conducted for passive candidates going through end to end process with a Talent Manager.  
  
What different terms mean \<\-\-\- this page will give you an insight of how to use the various evaluation and best practice.   


**Future State TE form**  
If we have urgent request please fill out the “TE sourcing form” and email to Dij Thulasi and CC in either Adam/Esha (Build) or Simon/Alicia (Local). Based on capacity we will review. This can be on the basis of YES or NO depending on capacity.   


**Inbound Applications ( FOR TE ANALYST)** 

In TE if you are looking after inbound applications \- For candidates you think are a good fit based on profile, you would need to validate this by calling the candidate. You would need to create a **recruiter evaluation** and contact the candidate via the phone if deemed right. 

You would need speak to the external candidate and if you deem them to be a **fit for end to end process →** you would then schedule an introduction via email to both candidate and TAM.   


**Sourcing Avenues** 

  
We will look to source from various avenues and forums. To date what has worked has been LinkedIn, Direct Inbound Applications and Referrals.   
  
Only qualified, vetted candidates added to JobScience (JS) – TE will make sure we have all relevant notes on **LinkedIn Profiles** of the candidates we have messaged – so please check if the candidate has been interacted with (this will be more accurate on LinkedIn). **Confidential information** won’t be added on the notes section of LinkedIn \- please add detailed notes on JobScience. We will put the relevant notes in place on their profile. Please **DO NOT** msg candidates 3x in a month. This is for both TAM/TE \- cadence of outreach msgs should be once every 3 months.   
  
A good candidate that **Applied Directly** for a role but no vacancy, TAM to send to TE. We will talent pool these candidates for future needs and put the appropriate information in place ← use the **Sourcing Evaluation Screen** if you’re pipelining.   
  
**Referrals Internally** to be sent to TE and we can give a great intro. 

**Passive candidate Engagement \- Please refer to the confluence page to an in\-depth information on how we engage –** <https://slalom.atlassian.net/l/cp/zGf912Sq>   


* **If a TE  does get an interest from a passive candidate, then we have a warm and engaging conversation. We will sell the Slalom brand and understand the candidates career aspiration. Part of this process we will try and establish if it comes naturally if the candidate is on a VISA.**  
  
**Note:** It won’t be a recruiter screen, we will just focus on selling Slalom as a brand and understand the candidate’s career aspiration and take the candidate through the next steps (meeting TAM).

**SLACK \~ adding candidates** 

  
We will then send this profile over to the TAM via Slack before adding on JS \- just to make it is in\-line. 

**We will @tag the TAM on slack and attach profile.**

This will be the format on slack

**Name:**

**Current role:**

**Overview:***(3 – 4 in dot point format)*

**Career Aspiration:***(2 – 3 dot point format)*

**Visa (optional) –**depending on the conversation

 

**The TAM can let us know if they are interested or not interested via this forum. If we are sure of a candidate being a fit, lets directly introduce them.**

**If TAM aren’t interested, TE can close this off with the candidate – talent pool them for future needs in accordance to fit. If the TAM are interested then we can make the introduction via email (this is a warm approach). But prior to the introduction via email, let's add them onto Jobscience (JS).**   


**JobScience \- Adding profiles** 

TE check database first to see if profile is on system. If not, we will add them onto JS by using ResumePower.  
  
On Resume Power use “source” as **LinkedIn Page** if we are sourcing via LinkedIn. Add candidates if they are deemed to be good into our dedicated shortlist. 

TE will then fill out the profile card details, skills and put the relevant notes/next step. We will add them part of the **“short cuts (talent pool)”** Shortlists/ MVC . TE add ourselves as the 'sourcer' and TAM as the 'recruiter' part of JS profile.

·      TE be responsible for adding all sourced passive candidates to JS going forward.   


We will be adding the “**branding”** and **“expanding the profile”**.

**\*\* In some cases, if it is hard to distinguish if the candidate is right for local/build, we will leave the TAM to send out the candidate portal.  Please notify to TAM via Slack \- this will allow TAM to determine after Screen.**   
  
Make sure TE put your notes onto JS as a **“Recruiter Evaluation” (if it is end to end process).** Candidate for Pipelining and future hire to be **“Sourcing Screen”.**

TAM \- Please action the candidate on Slack within 24 \- 48 hours if we ask you to review.  


TE do require an update on candidates we send across to the TAM in our catch ups. **We will do this in our meetings (collaboration or pipeline meeting).**  
  
**TAM to add “MVC”** Shortlists/ MVC  **on candidates aligned to our brand and fit on JS profile.**
