1) Brand Essence and Principles
We are people-first, uplifting, friendly, real, and innovative.
Voice is clear, confident, conversational.
Design and content should feel optimistic and human, not corporate or slick.
Prioritize authenticity and inclusivity in visuals and language.
2) Accessibility
Conform to WCAG 2.1 AA for all text, charts, and data visualizations.
Do not rely on color alone to convey meaning. Use color plus shape, pattern, and text labels.
Ensure minimum contrast ratios:
4.5:1 for body text (under ~18 px / 14 px bold).
3:1 for large text (≥ 24 px regular or ≥ 18.66 px bold).
Provide alt text for images and descriptive captions when helpful.
Coral Red and Chartreuse can be challenging for color blindness—use with care and always label data.
For charts, add labels and consider outlines or borders to increase readability.
3) Typography
Primary fonts for web:

Slalom Sans (primary; use in all branded materials except PPT)
Lora (supporting; optional for contrast or humanistic tone)
Avenir Next LT Pro is NOT permitted for web. It is reserved for PowerPoint and a few Microsoft exceptions only.
Usage and structure:

Limit to no more than three type sizes per layout.
Sentence case is standard for headlines and subheads.
All caps are rare: use sparingly for short eyebrows; never for body copy.
Alignment: Flush left, ragged right. Do not justify or center long text blocks.
Avoid widows, orphans, and hyphenation at line ends.
Weight and pairing:

Headlines: Slalom Sans Bold or Lora Regular
Subheads: Slalom Sans Bold or Lora Bold
Body: Slalom Sans Regular (prefer at smaller sizes and over images for legibility)
Spacing recommendations (brand guidance):

Leading (line-height):
Body copy: 1.1
Subheads: 0.90
All-cap subheads: 0.90
Headlines: 0.90
Tracking (letter-spacing):
Default: 0 for Slalom Sans
All-cap subheads: +75 (approx. +0.075em as an implementation starting point)
Ensure letters do not touch; adjust tracking only as needed.
Suggested internal web scale (adapt to your design system and test for accessibility):

H1: 40–48 px, line-height ~0.9
H2: 28–32 px, line-height ~0.9
H3: 22–24 px, line-height ~1.0
Body: 16–18 px, line-height ~1.1
Eyebrow (short all-caps): 12–14 px, tracking +0.075em, line-height ~0.9
Bullets:

Use round bullets. If body text is lightweight, use a heavier bullet for contrast.

5) Logo
Approved colors:

Slalom Blue, Slalom Dark Blue, White, Black
Do not use secondary colors for the logo.
Size and clear space:

Minimum digital height: 9 px (0.125 inches)
Clear space: at least the width of the letter “o” around the mark.
Do’s:

Place on colored, illustrated, or photographic backgrounds only when legibility is fully preserved.
On complex photos, align to a corner and ensure the logo does not block faces.
Don’ts:

No effects, rotations, skewing, 3D, recolors, or patterns.
Do not place on high-contrast or overly complex backgrounds that compromise legibility.
6) Iconography and Graphic Elements
Icons:

Functional yet friendly; simplest visual expression with unique Slalom style.
Pair icons with explanatory text; choose conceptually so they add meaning.
Preferred single-color icons: Slalom Blue or Slalom Dark Blue.
Colored circle backgrounds may be used for clarity on busy images; do not use Blue/Dark Blue circles.
Avoid overly detailed illustrations or generic stock icons; use approved Creative Library icons or match style if new.
Do not reverse colors (do not place light icons on dark backgrounds in a way that breaks brand rules).
Website-only icon set:

Use the dedicated web icon library for UI (arrows, chevrons, social, media controls, filters, search, etc.).
Maintain consistent sizing, hit areas, and accessibility labels for interactive icons.
Graphic elements and shapes:

Slalom shapes mix hard and soft angles; use shapes as frames and objects.
Only one rounded corner per rectangle; match curvature to the Slalom logo’s “S” curve.
Fluid waves reference brand flexibility and navigation; use sparingly and purposefully.
Color bars:

Represent inclusivity and brand palette.
Color order must be: Slalom Blue, Cyan, Coral Red, Purple, Chartreuse.
Use official EPS files; do not recreate.
Extend the framing Blue line to the full height or width of the layout.
Do not use multiple color bars in one layout or scale more than ~20% of layout height.
7) Photography and Video
Overall tone:

Positive, optimistic, joyful, vibrant, and saturated balanced by neutrals.
Real people and places; high quality, low pretension; candid expressions.
Do’s:

Compose genuine, candid scenes; subjects often unaware of the camera.
Show realistic depictions of technology and human interaction.
Be global-minded; represent diverse locations, cultures, and identities.
Choose unexpected, interesting photos that disprove stereotypes.
Use captions to highlight real employees when appropriate.
Don’ts:

Avoid images that are too “stock,” posed, or corporate.
Avoid homogeneity when showing multiple photos (e.g., one age range or skin tone).
Avoid overly dark, dreary, dystopian, or cyberpunk aesthetics.
People image styles:

Style 1: Slalom employees—authentic collaboration and core values.
Style 2: Global perspective—customers, partners, communities with cultural sensitivity.
Style 3: Portraits—capture in the moment; avoid stiff or corporate looks.
8) Writing Style for Web
Guides referenced:

Mostly follow The Chicago Manual of Style; Merriam-Webster for spelling.
Slalom writing style guide for deeper glossary and conventions.
Tone and approach:

Address readers directly; customer is the hero; be conversational, crisp, and clear.
Avoid jargon; define technical terms on first use; use active voice when possible.
Use metrics with sources where relevant; be consistent across pages.
Capitalization:

Sentence case for headlines and subheads across web and SharePoint.
Capitalize unique product/service/event names; use lowercase for generic descriptors.
Inclusive language:

Avoid biased language and problematic idioms; respect personal identity and pronouns.
Use singular “they” when appropriate.
Numbers and symbols:

Typically spell out zero to nine; use digits for 10+.
Use numerals and “%” (except when starting a sentence).
One space between sentences and after colons.
Terminology preferences:

“Slalomers” in informal internal contexts; “Slalom team members” in formal contexts.
Prefer “customers” over “clients” where applicable.
9) UI Elements: Practical Guidance
Buttons and links:

Primary actions use Slalom Blue; ensure AA contrast against backgrounds.
Hover/focus states must be clearly distinct and accessible (not color-only; include underline or shape/weight change).
Secondary actions use neutrals or one approved secondary color; never reduce contrast below AA.
Forms:

Labels and helper text in Slalom Sans Regular; maintain 4.5:1 contrast minimum.
Error and success states use color plus icons/text; do not rely on color alone.
Focus outlines must be visible and consistent across inputs.
Data visualizations:

Label axes and bars explicitly; use patterns or borders as needed for clarity.
Provide descriptive summaries or captions for complex charts.
